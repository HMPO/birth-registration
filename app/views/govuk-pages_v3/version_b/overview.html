What is registration

Births in England and Wales must be registered within 42 days (6 weeks). After 6 weeks the local council may contact you.

Registration is the official record of someone’s birth. Afterwards you can order birth certificates to apply for government services.

There are different rules for  registering a birth in Scotland,  registering a birth in Northern Ireland and  registering a birth abroad.

Information you provide

To register you’ll provide:

  • child’s name, date, and place of birth
  • birth mother’s name, place of birth, and job role
  • details of the father or birth mother’s female partner (if applicable)

How to register

Register online or in-person depending on if you are:

  • a parent recording both parents on the birth certificate
  • a birth mother recording only yourself on the birth certificate
  • not the child’s the parent
