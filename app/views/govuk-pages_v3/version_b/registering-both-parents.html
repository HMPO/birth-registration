Registering both parents

Married or civil-partner parents

Married parents can choose to:

  • register online - one parent can complete the online service
  • register in-person at a Register Office or Local Authority - only one parents needs to attend

Unmarried parents

Unmarried parents can either:

  • register online - the birth mother must start the process and invite the other parent
  • attend an appointment together - you’ll need to book an appointment

If you cannot attend together

One parent can attend in person if they have a document showing both parents approve, such as a court order or parenting agreement.

It might be possible to add the father’s details at a later date by completing an application for the re-registration of a child’s birth.

Children conceived with assisted conception (fertility treatment)

In UK legislation, the ‘mother’ is the person who gave birth to the child.

Unmarried parents must have received treatment:

  • at a licensed UK clinic
  • both parents were treated as a couple

Same-sex male couples

Male couples must get a parental order from the court before they can be registered as parents.

The mother (the person who gave birth to the child) must be recorded in the birth registration.
