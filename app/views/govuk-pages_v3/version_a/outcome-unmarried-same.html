Register a birth:  same-sex parents  (not married or in a civil partnership)

Registering online

In UK legislation, same-sex female parents be registered as:

  • the Mother (the person who gave birth to the child)
  • the Parent (the Mother’s female partner)

Parents must have received fertility treatment as a couple at a licensed UK clinic. You do not need to show evidence.

Birth mother needs to start the registration online, and will be given a unique link to share with father or second parent.

Both parents' identity will be checked by GOV.UK One Login. This works in a similar way to Government Gateway.

After you submit the form, a registrar at the council will complete the official registration.

Birth certificates will be sent by post. They cost £12.50 each (standard delivery).

The mother starts the online process to register a birth, and invites the father or parent to joint the registration.

Both parents will need to add their details separately.

Identity is checked by GOV.UK One Login, which works in a similar way to Government Gateway.

After you submit, a registrar at the council will complete the official registration. They’ll send certificates by post, usually within a week of registration.

In person registration

Unmarried parents must either:

  • attend the appointment together
  • one parent attends with a document showing both parents agree to registration

You’ll make an appointment at the register office for the area your child was born.

You do not need to attend with your child.

You’ll get birth certificates at the appointment. Each birth certificate costs £12.50.

In person registration

One parent can register if they have a legal document that shows you agree to be named as the child’s parents, or a parenthood agreement from the fertility clinic.

It might be possible to add the father’s details at a later date by completing an application for the re-registration of a child’s birth.
