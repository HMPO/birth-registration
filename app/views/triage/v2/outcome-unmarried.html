Registering a child with parents who are not married

You can register a child online, or in person at a register office.

Registering online

Either parent can start the online process to register a birth. Both parents will electronically sign the registration.

Identity is checked by GOV.UK One Login, which works in a similar way to Government Gateway.

After you provide the information:

  • a local register office will check the information
  • a registrar will add the child’s birth to the register
  • birth certificates are sent by post

Registering in person

You’ll make an appointment at the register office for the area your child was born.

Both parents must attend the same appointment. Otherwise you cannot both be named as parents on the birth certificate.

You do not need to take your child.

If you cannot attend together

One parent can register if you get a parental responsibility agreement or court order.
