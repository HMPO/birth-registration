Register a birth: parents who are not married (or in civil partnership)

Births in England and Wales must be registered within 42 days (6 weeks). After 6 weeks the local council may contact you.

Registering online

Both parents will sign in to the online service to provide the information to the birth registration.

The mother must start and submit the online application to:

  • provide their and their child’s details
  • invite the father or parent to join the registration
  • confirm final details are ready for review at the register office
  • order any birth certificates (£12.50 each, including delivery by post)

    • During the process the {father/parent} will:

    • be invited to join the online service
    • provide their own details
    • check they agree with the information provided by the mother

    How long it takes

    It takes up to 3 weeks for register offices to:

    • check the application
    • officially register the birth
    • print and send birth certificates (delivery time depends on the register office)

    In person registration

    You'll make an appointment at the register office for the area your child was born in.

    Unmarried parents must usually attend the appointment together.

    One parent can register alone if they have legal document that shows both parents agree to the registration.

    • attend the appointment together

    You do not need to attend with your child.
