Overview

Births in England and Wales must be registered (online or in-person) within 42 days (6 weeks). After 6 weeks the local council may contact you.

You can get a birth certificate after registration. Use the birth certificate to apply for government services, such as first passport application.

There are different rules for  registering a birth in Scotland,  registering a birth in Northern Ireland and  registering a birth abroad.

Information you need when registering a birth

To register you’ll provide:

  • child’s name, date of birth and NHS number  
  • birth mother’s details
  • father or birth mother’s female partner’s details (optional)

How to register

Both parents can register the birth. In some cases, you can register the birth if you’re not the child’s parent.

The process is different depending on if you:

  • include both parents details
  • include only the mother (the birth mother) on the birth certificate

Female couples can include both their names on their child’s birth certificate when registering the birth.

Start now

You can also book appointment

Birth needs to be register in the local register office. Which office you should go to depends on where the child was born.

To book an appointment find you local register office.
