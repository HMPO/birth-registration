Registering both parents

Registering online

Married or civil-partner parents

Either parent can register the birth of your child online if you are married to each other (including civil partnerships).

One parent can provide the details for the registration online. Afterwards, it’s checked and approved by a registrar (at local council Register Office).

Unmarried parents

Parents who are not married must attend an in-person registration appointment together.

One parent get can attend on their own if they have a document showing the other parent agrees to be recorded on the birth certificate. For example:

  • court order 
  • parental agreement 
  • evidence of treatment as a couple at a licensed fertility clinic 

It might be possible to add the father’s details at a later date by completing an for the re-registration of a child’s birth.

Registering in person

Births in England and Wales must be registered (online or in-person) within 42 days (6 weeks). After 6 weeks the local council may contact you.

Find the register office by post code look up.

If the parents are married, only one parents parent needs to attend the appointment (with ID). You do not need to bring your child.

Parents who are not married must attend the appointment together.

Birth certificate given at the appointment. They cost £12 each.  

Same-sex male couples

Male couples must get a parental order from the court before they can be registered as parents.
