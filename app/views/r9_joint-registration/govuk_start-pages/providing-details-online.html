Registering a birth (England and Wales)

Contents

  1. Providing details online
  2. Getting birth certificates

Births in England and Wales must be registered within 42 days (6 weeks). After 6 weeks the local council may contact you.

Registration is the official record of someone’s birth. Afterwards you can order birth certificates to apply for government services.

Providing details online

Married parents

Either parent can complete the online service if they were married to each other at the time of the birth.

Or at the time you got pregnant, if you are same sex couple.

Unmarried parents

The child’s mother - the person who gave birth to the baby - must start the process and invite the other parent.

In registration, the other parent is registered as either:

  • the father - the biological father or father to a child conceived with fertility treatment
  • the parent - the mother’s female partner who conceived together with fertility treatment

Information you provide

You’ll verify your identity with GOV.UK One Login. You can sign in or create an account to use the service.

A birth registration includes information about:

  • the child - name, date, and place of birth
  • the mother - name, place of birth, and job role
  • the father or parent - name, place of birth, and job role
